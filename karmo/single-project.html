Cube House and Wedding Pavilion on Lake Erie

Fall '16 || 48-100 Design Studio || Professors G. Damiani and A. Moss

This project required the design of a cube shaped pavilion to host weddings on the East shore of Lake Erie. Working on a site of 80' x 80', the site included the 20' x 20' pavilion, a viewing garden, and reception area. Several utility spaces were also included, such as a bathroom, storage shed, and food service area. All spaces were carved out of a block of hedges, measuring 80' x 80' x 8'.

The anchor of all of the spaces on the site was the Cube House. An elevated platform served as a private area for the bride and groom to prepare, while also giving a spectacular view out over the lake and site. The couple would be married on the main floor, set against the lake as a majestic background. My bathroom was also included in a back corner of the pavilion, as to not impede on the relative natural beauty of the site. The stairs to the elevated platform wrapped around the outside facade of the cube. The stairs did not have connecting risers and were extruded directly out of the concrete of the house so those approaching the house down the path could glimpse views of the lake, enticing them to approach.

All spaces included in the site were facilitated through the boundries provided by the Cube. My main goal was to create a dynamic site with clear circulation throughout. The use of the natural beauty of the site was also extremely influental in the carving out of the spaces. Before entering the site, the lake would only be visible through the entrance. However, once within the site, all spaces had direct views of the lake and setting sun.

Evan Lehner || Architecture Student || Carnegie Mellon University
